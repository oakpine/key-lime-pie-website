The History of Key Lime Pie

The Classic Southern Dessert is from...New York?

That's right! Although there is no definitive answer as to where Key Lime Pie originates, there is strong evidence that shows that it could be originally from New York! Research shows that there was no Key Lime Pie or similar dessert recipe in the Florida region (it used to be widely believed that Key Lime Pie originated from Florida, as Key Lime Pie is Florida's state dessert) before 1949. However, there was a recipe that is very similar to Key Lime Pie that was dated back to 1931. The recipe found in New York originally used lemons instead of limes. It is now believed that this New York recipe made its way to the southern states, such as Florida, and the lemon was replaced with the regional key lime, creating the dessert that we all (especially Florida natives) know and love today!


Not All Agree...

While there is proof to show that the Key Lime Pie originated from NYC, Florida natives and other southerners are not too keen on the idea that their beloved dessert may have not come from the south. Floridians have dug up evidence that could support the idea that Key Lime Pie came from Florida. There was a woman who went by the name of "Aunt Sally" who worked in the Curry Mansion, which is in Key West, Florida. Records have shown that Aunt Sally did exist back in 1890, which is also the year that Aunt Sally supposedly created the Key Lime Pie recipe. She may have had access to ingredients like sweetened condensed milk in 1890, making the history of this dessert more ambiguous.

No Matter the History, Key Lime Pie is Delicious!
