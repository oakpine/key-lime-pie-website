Granulated Sugar

Why Granulated Sugar?

Granulated sugar gives the filling of a key lime pie a sweet taste. It also can be added to the crust of a key lime pie for an extra depth of flavor. Granulated sugar also dissolves more easily than other sugars, so it can be easily incorporated into any aspect of the dessert. The sweetness balances out the tartness of the limes.

Where does it come from?

Granulated sugar can come from two different plants. It can either come from the sugar beet or the sugar cane. Read more to learn about these plants.

Sugar Cane

Sugar canes are a very popular source for the sugar that we use everyday. It spread all around the world due to slave labor, but it is native to Asia. In the US, you can find it in southern states such as Florida, Louisiana, Hawaii, and Texas

Sugar Beet

Sugar beets are native to the US. It is often found in the north in states such as Oregon and Idaho. It can also be found in Wyoming, Colorado, Montana, North Dakota, Northern Minnesota, Northeastern Michigan, Western Nebraska, and Southern California. Sugar beets are different than regular beets due to their size and color.
