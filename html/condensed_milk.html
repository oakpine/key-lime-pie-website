Sweetened Condensed Milk


Condensed milk is made by removing most of the water from a cow's milk, resulting in a dense, syrupy liquid. Sugar is also added, giving the condensed cow's milk a sweet taste and allowing for a longer shelf life. The liquid is collected and canned.

This product was originally developed in France, but it began to spread to other places. Now, it can be made and bought anywhere in the world.
